Overview

History of Football

Beginnings

The first known ball game which also involved kicking took place In China in the 2nd and 3rd century BC under the name cuju. Cuju was played with a round ball (stitched leather with fur or feathers inside) on an area of a square. A modified form of this game later spread to Japan and was by the name of kemari practiced under ceremonial forms.

Mid Era

Football in its current form arose in England in the middle of the 19th century. The sport was at first entertainment for the British working class. The game was soon expanded by British people that traveled to other parts of the world. Football clubs have existed since the 15th century, but they were unrecognized and without official status. Federation International Football Association (FIFA) was founded in 1904. This game is slowly gaining in popularity but can be no match yet to the original game of football.

Player Positions

A football team consists of 11 players which includes Fowards, Midfielders, Defenders and a Goalkeeper to defend the goal. Each role has different objectives that contribute towards the game. Players are positioned in different formations depending on either an attacking or defending approach. Player positions and their roles are more deeply analyzed to get a better understanding below.

Forward

Forwards, also known as strikers are players positioned nearest to the opposition's goal. The main objective of forwards is to score goals and assist scoring chances. Usually a team consists of 1 to 3 forwards depending on the formation.

Centre Forward-CF

The main target of a Centre forward is to score goals as much as possible. This player should be able to win the ball from opposition and attempt to score by either using the feet or head. A team usually consist of either one or two CFs.

Second Striker

Second striker or support striker is a player who plays between the defenders and mid-fielders of the opposition. This player should have the ability to be fast and skillfull enough to create scoring chances for CFs or score themselves.

Winger

A winger is a player placed in a wide position of the pitch, usually playing the left winger or the right winger role. A wingers characteristic is to dribble past opponents with speed and create scoring chances for the CF or to create wide attacking chances.

Mid Fielder

Midfielders are players whose position is between forwards and defenders. Their main objective is to maintain possession of the ball, and pass onto forwards.

Central Middfielder

Central midfielders have the ability to perform both forward and defending roles making them an important role in the game, known as Attacking midfielder and Defensive midfielder.

Attacking Midfielder

An Attacking midfielder is positioned inbetween forwards and the rest of the midfielders. Attacking midfield can be divided into right,left and central attacking midfield roles depending on the formation. Their primary objective is an offensive role.

Defensive Midfielder

A Defensive midfielder is positioned beteween defenders and the central midfield. they are used to provide more defensive protection, helping defensive players by advancing towards opposition forwards and midfielders.

Defender

Defenders are positioned behind the Midfielders and their primary objective is to provide support and help defend the goal by preventing the opposition from scoring a goal.

Centre Back

Centre backs are positioned in the centre area near the penalty box and their main objective is to defend against the strikes and to take away the ball from the penalty area.

Full Back

Full back defenders consist of left-back and right-back which stand either side of the centre back to defend against wide attack plays.

Wing Back

Wing backs, just like attaking mid-fielders are defenders whom generates attack plays from the defensive line. Players who take on this role must have high stamina as they have to run back and forth to switch roles.

Goal Keeper

Goalkeeper is positioned in the penalty area to protect the goal. Their main duty is to stop the opposition from scoring goals by catching, palming, punching or kicking the ball from on target attacking shots. Goalkeeper is the only player allowed to handle the ball which is restricted to their penalty area.

Goalkeepers wear a different coloured kit compared to their teammates and also wears gloves to provide more grip on the ball and to protect their hand. Some goalkeepers even wear a headgaurd to protect from blows.

Goalkeeper, unlike other roles is the only irreplaceable role in football, meaning if a goalkeeper gets injured, he must be replaced by a substitute goalkeeper or if he gets sent off, a player in the outfield must take on the goalkeeper duties.

What is a Football League?

Football league is an association of football clubs that organizes matches between member teams of a similar standard. Usually teams will play each other in a round robin set up, points are awarded, 3 points for a win, 1 point for a draw and 0 points for a lose. They are governed by set of rules decided by founding clubs, usually in line with country FA rules, then with FIFA rules.
